Bike Barn

As a motorcycle safety instructor I see our fleet always in a state of flux. Communication between the garage and the office can be unpredictable. It seemed that a tool to store all the vitals of a bike existed in a simple excel spreadsheet. But that spreadsheet had no connection to the life of the bike as chronicled in notebooks in the garage or word of mouth and memory. So I set out to bridge the gap.

Bike Barn is designed to help motorcycle training facilities, and enthusiasts with a full garage, connect and share all the data on their machines, whether sitting at their desk or standing next to a motorcycle in their garage. It lets anyone check the status of any bike in the fleet at any time.

    Bike Barn has:

  • A page that has all the information on every bike in a sortable list. From color to VIN.

  • Two abbreviated lists with only essential data, one for ready bikes, one for offline bikes.

  • A validated form for entering all pertinent data on a new machine.

  • A maintenance log that can be called up by selecting any bike on any list.

  • A quick overview of bike readiness, year and type.

  • Mobile versions of pages that would be useful in the garage

Lists are sortable for easy access to desired data. The complete maintenance history on each motorcycle can be seen from anywhere. And any bike can be marked as ready or offline with a touch.

All data is stored in an offsite, secure, synchronized database.

Bike-barn is expandable, with room for more data fields if needed. And there’s a framework pre-built for a parts database. It’s primed for expansion.
